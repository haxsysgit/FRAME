# Scoring And Weights

Scoring is how the lab decides whether a candidate is actually improving.

## Tiny idea

A schema can feel cleaner while still being worse.
Scoring helps stop that kind of self-deception.

## What the current scoring cares about

The active 0.2.0 line scores around three broad phases:
- structural fit
- interaction usefulness
- pressure and cost

That includes things like:
- clarity
- name clarity
- cross-link quality
- retrieval usefulness
- governance/usefulness of proof and review structure
- token efficiency
- stability
- adoptability

## Why the scoring changed

Older scoring was useful, but 0.2.0 needed more direct pressure on:
- naming simplicity
- typed cross-link quality
- retrieval usefulness
- whether Acts was helping future work cycles

## Current active result

From the active audited 0.2.0 round:
- organize: 4.25 / 5
- autopahe: 4.35 / 5
- pharmax: 4.49 / 5
- weighted average: 4.36 / 5

That does not mean FRAME is final.
It means the current line is stronger than the older one.
